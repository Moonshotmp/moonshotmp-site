Men’s Hormone Optimization Pathways

Hormone optimization for men is a medically guided process focused on evaluating and managing testosterone levels in the context of symptoms, health history, and long-term wellbeing.

Decisions are based on comprehensive bloodwork with ongoing monitoring and medical oversight. There is no single "right" protocol; care is adjusted over time based on labs and how you feel.

TESTOSTERONE INJECTIONS

How it works
  • Bioidentical testosterone administered via injection
  • Typically dosed twice per week
  • Can be self-administered at home or given in-clinic
What to expect
  • Flexible dosing that can be adjusted over time
  • Requires consistent injections on a weekly schedule
  • Bloodwork monitored at regular intervals to ensure safety and effectiveness
Good fit for
  • Patients who prefer flexibility
  • Those comfortable with injections
  • Individuals who want easier dose adjustments

TESTOSTERONE PELLETS

How it works
  • Brief in-office procedure (approximately 5 minutes)
  • Small incision in the upper buttocks
  • Bioidentical testosterone pellets inserted based on height, weight, labs, and symptoms
What to expect
  • Slow, steady release of testosterone over approximately 5 months
  • No weekly or daily dosing
  • Return every ~5 months for pellet replacement
  • Bloodwork monitored regularly and dosage adjusted at future insertions as needed
Additional Note
  • One-time initial pellet insertion fee: $215
  • After the initial insertion, there are no additional pellet procedure charges beyond the standard monthly hormone optimization program

MONITORING & ADJUSTMENTS

Both pathways include regular bloodwork. Care begins with a conservative dose, which can be adjusted up or down based on labs and symptoms. All changes are reviewed by licensed medical providers. The goal is supporting how you feel while prioritizing long-term safety, not chasing numbers.
