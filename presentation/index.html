The Timeline Nobody Tells You

Perimenopause

4-10 years. Can start late 30s, typically mid-40s.

Progesterone drops FIRST.

Menopause

12 months without a period. Average age 51.

Estrogen at 10-20% of peak.

Postmenopause

Everything after. Ongoing management.

Why Your Doctor Says "You're Fine"

Most people aren't being misdiagnosed — they're being under-measured.

1

Testing Is Minimal by Design

Standard care screens for disease, not function. TSH alone. Maybe estradiol. Rarely progesterone. Almost never testosterone.

If you only check one gauge, you'll miss most performance problems.

2

"Normal" Is Average, Not Optimal

Normal ranges include sedentary, stressed, metabolically unhealthy, and elderly populations.

"Normal" just means you're average — not that you're thriving.

3

Default Solutions Are Symptom Suppressors

Fatigue → stimulants. Mood → antidepressants. Sleep → sedatives. Low drive → "that's just life."

That's not bad medicine — it's limited medicine.

The Study That Changed Everything

The Headline (2002)

26%

"increased breast cancer risk"

vs

The Actual Number

0.08%

8 additional cases per 10,000/year

Three problems: Wrong population (avg age 63) · Wrong hormones (horse estrogen + synthetic progestin) · Wrong route (oral, not transdermal)

The Science Has Moved On

1

18-Year JAMA Follow-Up (2017)

No significant mortality difference

2

Timing Hypothesis

Starting HRT within 10 years of menopause or before 60 = cardiovascular benefit

3

Major Medical Societies Endorse HRT

NAMS, Endocrine Society now endorse HRT for symptomatic women under 60

Delivery Methods

Transdermal Estradiol

Patches, creams, gels. Most common starting point.

Oral Progesterone

Bedtime dosing. Sleep-promoting via GABA pathway.

Testosterone Cream

Small daily dose. Energy, libido, body composition.

Pellets

Subcutaneous implant. 3-5 month steady release. No daily dosing.

Vaginal Estrogen

Local treatment for urogenital symptoms.
