RECOVERY & OPTIMIZATION

Supportive tools used within a data-driven performance medicine framework.

WHAT RECOVERY & OPTIMIZATION MEANS

Recovery and optimization therapies are supportive tools used to help the body adapt, recover, and function more effectively — when guided by data and clinical context.

At Moonshot Medical & Performance, these therapies are never used in isolation and are not offered as walk-in or one-off services. They are incorporated only when they support a broader, medically guided performance plan.

This is not a wellness bar. And it’s not a shortcut.

It’s optimization with intention.

WHY RECOVERY REQUIRES CONTEXT

Recovery is often treated as something to add on. In reality, recovery is a signal problem, not a volume problem.

Low energy, slow healing, poor sleep, or limited adaptation can stem from metabolic stress, hormonal imbalance, or nutrient insufficiency.

Without understanding why recovery is limited, adding tools often creates noise instead of progress. That’s why every tool we use is anchored to data.

THIS APPROACH MAY BE APPROPRIATE IF...

  • ✓ You are already engaging in performance medicine or diagnostics.
  • ✓ Recovery, resilience, or adaptation feels limited.
  • ✓ You want tools chosen based on data, not trends.
  • ✓ You prefer clinician guidance over trial-and-error.
  • ✓ You care about long-term outcomes, not quick boosts.

THIS MAY NOT BE A FIT IF...

  • × You want walk-in vitamin shots or IV menus.
  • × You’re looking for cosmetic or spa-style services.
  • × You want treatments without evaluation or follow-up.
  • × You’re seeking immediate results without context.

We intentionally avoid transactional care.

THE MOONSHOT RECOVERY FRAMEWORK

All recovery and optimization decisions follow the same clinical loop:

01

MEASURE

We assess relevant diagnostics, symptoms, and trends to understand recovery capacity.

02

INTERPRET

We determine what’s limiting adaptation — not just what’s missing.

03

OPTIMIZE

Supportive tools are introduced only when appropriate and in alignment with your physiology.

04

TRACK

We monitor response over time and adjust based on outcomes, not assumptions. Recovery without tracking is guessing.

TOOLS WE MAY USE

Peptide-Based Support

Peptides may be used to support tissue recovery, healing processes, training adaptation, and resilience under stress.

Peptides are selected and monitored within a medical framework — not used as performance shortcuts.

Shockwave Therapy (Recovery-Focused)

Shockwave may be incorporated as a non-invasive recovery modality to support tissue health, circulation, musculoskeletal recovery, and localized healing.

It is used selectively and never positioned as a standalone solution.

Targeted Micronutrient & Vitamin Support

In appropriate cases, we may recommend vitamin injections, targeted micronutrient repletion, and clinician-directed supplementation.

These are supportive, not primary therapies, and are chosen based on documented need.

Clinician-Recommended Supplements

Supplement strategies are intentional, minimal, and data-driven.

We do not sell supplement stacks for the sake of selling supplements.

WHAT PROGRESS OFTEN LOOKS LIKE

Recovery and optimization is rarely dramatic — and that’s a good thing. Patients often report:

✓ Improved recovery between training or stress cycles
✓ Steadier energy throughout the day
✓ Better sleep quality
✓ Fewer setbacks or plateaus
✓ Greater confidence in how their body responds

The goal is not stimulation.
It’s capacity.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Are these services available on their own?

No. Recovery and optimization tools are used as part of a broader performance medicine plan and are not offered as standalone, walk-in services.

Is this the same as IV therapy or a vitamin bar?

No. Those models focus on convenience and volume. Our approach focuses on clinical context, diagnostics, and long-term outcomes.

Do peptides replace other treatments?

No. Peptides are supportive tools. They do not replace foundational medical care, diagnostics, or lifestyle factors.

How do you decide which tools to use?

Decisions are based on symptoms, lab data, response patterns, and safety considerations — not trends.

Is this covered by insurance?

Most recovery and optimization therapies are not covered by insurance. We are transparent about this upfront.

How long does it take to notice changes?

Timelines vary. Some recovery improvements are noticed within weeks, while others emerge gradually as the system adapts.

GETTING STARTED

Recovery and optimization should be earned, not sold.

If you’re already engaged in performance medicine — or are ready to begin with diagnostics — we’ll determine whether recovery-focused tools are appropriate for you.

Clarity first. Tools second. Progress over time.
