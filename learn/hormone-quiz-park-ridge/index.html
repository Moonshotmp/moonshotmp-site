WHY HORMONE SYMPTOMS TRAVEL TOGETHER

Hormonal decline doesn't cause one symptom. It causes patterns. Understanding your pattern is the first step toward fixing it.

Men: Testosterone Decline

Testosterone affects energy, cognition, mood, sleep, muscle, fat storage, and sexual function. When it declines — starting around age 30 at roughly 1% per year — multiple systems are affected simultaneously. That's why "tired, foggy, gaining weight, low drive" isn't four problems. It's one.

Common patterns: fatigue + recovery, brain fog + mood, body changes + physical symptoms.

Women: Estrogen, Progesterone & Testosterone

Women's hormone decline involves multiple hormones shifting at different rates. Progesterone often drops first (disrupting sleep and mood), followed by estrogen (hot flashes, brain fog, vaginal/bladder changes), and testosterone (energy, libido, muscle loss). The combination creates overlapping symptoms that get dismissed as "stress" or "aging."

Common patterns: sleep + mood, hot flashes, body + sexual + bladder changes.

COMMON QUESTIONS

How do I know if my symptoms are hormonal?

Hormonal symptoms tend to cluster in recognizable patterns: fatigue paired with brain fog, sleep problems with mood changes, weight gain with muscle loss. Our free quiz evaluates 8 symptom categories and identifies which pattern matches you. It's not a diagnosis — but it tells you whether blood work is worth pursuing.

Is the quiz a medical diagnosis?

No. The quiz is an educational screening tool that helps identify symptom patterns consistent with hormonal imbalance. Blood work is the only way to confirm hormone levels and determine whether treatment is appropriate. The quiz helps you decide whether testing is a reasonable next step.

For men: total and free testosterone, SHBG, estradiol, LH, FSH, full thyroid panel, cortisol, CBC, metabolic panel, vitamin D, and B12.
