COMMON QUESTIONS

What are the common side effects of GLP-1 medications?

The most common side effects are GI-related: nausea, constipation, and decreased appetite (which is partly how they work). These are usually mild and improve as your body adjusts. Starting at low doses and titrating up gradually helps minimize side effects. Your clinician monitors and adjusts as needed.

Will I regain the weight if I stop the medication?

Studies show significant weight regain after stopping GLP-1 medications if nothing else changes. That's why our approach focuses on preserving muscle (via DEXA tracking), building sustainable habits, and addressing underlying metabolic issues. The goal is to eventually taper or discontinue with a strong metabolic foundation.

Am I a candidate for GLP-1 weight loss?

GLP-1 medications are appropriate for adults with a BMI of 30+ (or 27+ with weight-related health conditions). We evaluate each patient individually with comprehensive blood work and health history. Not everyone needs medication—sometimes optimizing hormones, thyroid, or metabolic function is the real fix.

Can I combine GLP-1 with hormone therapy?

Yes, and it's often beneficial. Optimizing testosterone or thyroid function alongside GLP-1 medication can improve muscle preservation and metabolic outcomes. We coordinate all treatments as a single integrated protocol based on your full lab picture.
