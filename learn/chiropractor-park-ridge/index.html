WHY MOONSHOT IS DIFFERENT

Most chiropractic offices are built on a maintenance model—keep you coming back indefinitely. Moonshot is built on the opposite: get you better and get you out.

The Maintenance Problem

Many chiropractic clinics lock you into long treatment plans with 3x/week adjustments for months. You feel temporary relief after each visit, but nothing changes long-term. The adjustment becomes the product—not the outcome.

The result: You stay dependent on adjustments instead of learning how to manage and resolve the problem yourself.

The Moonshot Approach

Dr. Gontarek uses the McKenzie Method—one of the most researched approaches in spinal rehabilitation. Every visit combines manual therapy with corrective exercises and patient education. You learn what's causing the problem and what to do about it on your own.

The result: Most patients see significant improvement within 6-8 visits. The goal is graduation, not dependency.

WHAT TO EXPECT AT YOUR FIRST VISIT

1

Comprehensive Evaluation (45-60 Minutes)

Your first visit is not a quick crack-and-go. Dr. Gontarek takes 45-60 minutes to assess your movement patterns, pain behavior, medical history, and functional limitations. This is a full mechanical assessment—not a surface-level exam followed by a standard adjustment.

2

Mechanical Classification

Using the McKenzie Method, your pain is classified by how it responds to specific movements and positions. This tells us whether your problem is a derangement, dysfunction, or postural syndrome—and determines the exact treatment approach. No guesswork, no generic protocols.

3

Treatment + Education

Treatment combines hands-on manual therapy with corrective exercises you can do at home. You leave every visit understanding why you're doing each exercise and how it addresses your specific problem. This isn't a passive experience—you become an active participant in your recovery.

4

Clear Treatment Plan

You get a concrete plan: how many visits, what milestones to track, and what "done" looks like. If additional services would accelerate recovery—dry needling, shockwave therapy, trigger point injections, or IASTM—Dr. Gontarek will recommend them with clear reasoning.

CONDITIONS WE TREAT

Back Pain

Lower back pain is the most common reason patients visit. Whether it's acute (sudden onset) or chronic (lingering for months), the McKenzie assessment identifies the mechanical pattern driving your symptoms and the specific movements that reduce them.

Neck Pain

Desk work, phone posture, and poor ergonomics are behind most neck pain. We address the root cause—not just the symptom—through mechanical assessment, manual therapy, and corrective exercises that create lasting change.

Headaches

Cervicogenic headaches originate from neck dysfunction. When the cervical spine is the driver, chiropractic care is one of the most effective treatments. We identify whether your headaches have a mechanical origin and treat accordingly.

Sciatica

Pain radiating down the leg is often driven by a disc derangement in the lower spine. The McKenzie Method is particularly effective for sciatica—identifying the directional preference that centralizes and reduces radiating symptoms.

Joint Pain

Shoulder, hip, and knee pain often have mechanical causes that respond to targeted manual therapy and corrective exercise. We assess the joint, identify contributing factors, and build a plan to restore function.

Sports Injuries

From weekend warriors to competitive athletes, musculoskeletal injuries respond to the same evidence-based principles: accurate diagnosis, targeted treatment, and progressive loading. We get you back to training—not just out of pain.

What is the McKenzie Method and why does it matter?

The McKenzie Method (Mechanical Diagnosis and Therapy) is an evidence-based assessment and treatment system for spinal and extremity pain. It classifies your pain by how it responds to specific movements, identifies the exercises that reduce your symptoms, and teaches you how to manage the problem independently. It's one of the most researched approaches in physical rehabilitation. Learn more about our chiropractic approach.
