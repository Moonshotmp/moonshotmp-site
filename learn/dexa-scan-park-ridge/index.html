WHAT YOUR SCALE CAN'T TELL YOU

Your scale tells you one number: total weight. That number says nothing about what's actually changing inside your body.

The Scale Problem

You can lose 10 lbs of muscle and gain 10 lbs of fat and your scale says nothing changed. You can gain 5 lbs of muscle and lose 5 lbs of fat and your scale says nothing changed. Weight alone is useless for tracking real progress.

Smart Scales & InBody

Bioelectrical impedance (what smart scales and InBody use) estimates body composition based on how electricity passes through your body. Hydration, meals, and exercise can swing results 3-5%. That's noise, not data. See our full comparison.

DEXA: The Gold Standard

DEXA uses dual-energy X-ray to directly measure fat, lean tissue, and bone. Less than 1% error between scans. It shows you exactly where fat and muscle are distributed—not just a single body-fat number.

WHAT YOUR DEXA REPORT SHOWS

Total Body Fat %

Your overall body fat percentage, measured directly—not estimated. The most accurate reading available outside of a research lab.

Regional Muscle Mass

Lean mass in each arm, each leg, and your trunk. Identifies imbalances and tracks muscle gain from training or TRT.

Visceral Adipose Tissue (VAT)

The fat around your organs—the most dangerous kind. Strongly linked to metabolic disease, diabetes, and cardiovascular risk. No other consumer method measures this accurately.

Bone Mineral Density

Identifies osteopenia or osteoporosis risk early. Important for women post-menopause and men on certain medications.

Fat Distribution Map

See exactly where your body stores fat. Android (belly) vs. gynoid (hip/thigh) distribution has different health implications.

Scan-to-Scan Tracking

Compare scans over time to see exactly how many pounds of fat you lost and muscle you gained. Real data, not guesswork.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Is the radiation from a DEXA scan safe?

Yes. A DEXA scan exposes you to about 1-4 microsieverts of radiation—roughly equivalent to a few hours of natural background radiation or eating a couple of bananas. It's far less radiation than a standard chest X-ray.

What should I wear for a DEXA scan?

Comfortable athletic clothing without metal (no zippers, buttons, or underwire bras). Remove jewelry and anything with metal. The more consistent your clothing between scans, the more accurate your comparisons.

Can I get a DEXA scan if I'm pregnant?

No. DEXA scans are not performed on pregnant women due to radiation exposure, even though the dose is very low. If you think you may be pregnant, let us know before scheduling.

Do I need to fast before a DEXA scan?

Not required, but for the most consistent results between scans, we recommend scanning at a similar time of day and hydration state. If you're bundling with blood work, you'll be fasting anyway.
